New York Is Coming for 7-OH — And Maybe Kratom Too

NEW YORK · 7-OH, Kratom · Mar 15, 2026 · 4 sources

New York has multiple bills in committee right now targeting 7-OH and kratom from every angle — full Schedule I ban, 2% threshold cap, and a straight ban on any food or drink containing either substance.

The one gaining traction: S8686 would ban all kratom-infused foods and beverages starting January 2027. Fines start at $500. A separate bill would raise the minimum purchase age to 21. And the most aggressive one would throw kratom onto Schedule I — same category as heroin.

This comes right behind the FDA's push to schedule 7-OH federally, which has been lighting a fire under state legislatures nationwide. New Jersey, Mississippi, and Louisiana have already moved. New York with its 4,100+ smoke shops could be next.
