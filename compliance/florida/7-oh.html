Florida — 7-OH (7-Hydroxymitragynine)

Last updated: March 14, 2026 Banned

Legal Status

Concentrated 7-OH products are banned in Florida under an emergency scheduling order. Products containing more than 2% 7-hydroxymitragynine by weight are classified as Schedule I controlled substances.

Natural kratom leaf products containing trace amounts of 7-OH (under 2%) remain legal for adults 21 and over with labeling requirements.

Penalties: First offense civil fine of $1,000. Subsequent violations up to $5,000 per occurrence. Criminal penalties apply for large-scale distribution.

Key bill: FL Emergency Order §893.03 · FL Dept. of Health
